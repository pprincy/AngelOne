Online IPO - Product Features

Essential information
  1. Angel Broking online IPO product allows you to conveniently apply for public issues both fixed price & Book building and eliminates the need for filling out tedious/detailed application
  2. An issue is an offer of shares by either through the Fixed Price mechanism or Book Building process.
  3. In case the offer of shares is made through the book building process (determined by the number of shares applied for and the price thereof), the allotment price is determined by the Book Running
    Lead manager(s) /SEBI based on the bids submitted.
  4. You are requested to read the issuer's offer document/ prospectus carefully before submitting an application/bid.
Key points
  1. The product is offered only to Individuals, NRI (NRE/NRO) and HUF who have executed & signed the Power of Attorney and Client Member Agreement with Angel Broking Private Limited (the company).
  2. Bids received by us will be routed only through the National Stock Exchange (NSE) / Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE).
  3. In case the offer of shares is made through the book building process (determined by the number of shares applied for and the price thereof), the allotment price is determined by the Book Running Lead manager(s) /SEBI based on the bids submitted.
  4. Incase of fixed price issues, the application would be sent to the Registrar to the Issue and Bankers Issue.
  5. You can apply through the ASBA option. ASBA (Application Supported by Blocked Amounts) option is a process developed by the Securities and Exchange Board Of India (SEBI) for applying to IPO. In ASBA; an IPO applicant's account doesn't get debited until shares are allotted to him.
  6. Application/bid will be sent to the concerned Registrars to the Issue of Issuer companies in the name(s) and sequential order as appearing in the Demat account of the applicant (s) (While making an application/bid, you can choose the Demat a/c where you would like the allotment of shares if any, to happen post closure of the relevant IPO).
  7. The communication/correspondence address on the application/bid will be the same as available in the investors/applicant(s)/bidder(s) demat account.
Note:
  1. The receipt of an application/bid by us does not in any way confirm and or guarantee that the shares applied will be allotted to the applicant/bidder.
  2. In case of trading account in the name of sole proprietor, the application must be in the name/s of the applicant/s of the Demat a/c holder/s (as chosen by you at the time of application).

Placing an IPO order

To place an IPO order:
  1. Place your IPO order from the IPO Home page or Place Order screen displayed on the website of the company.
  2. Select an issue and click on Apply link on IPO Home Page or Proceed on Place Order screen.
  3. Select a category (Retail/Non Institutional/ASBA Retail/ASBA Non Institution) for the Resident category. The issue's details are displayed.
  4. Select a category (NRE Retail/NRO Retail/ASBA NRO Retail/ASBA NRE Retail/Non Institution NRE/Non Institution NRO/ASBA NRO Non Institution/ASBA NRE Non Institution) for NRI category. The issue's details are displayed.
  5. Enter the necessary bid details
    1. Place your IPO order from the IPO Home page or Place Order screen displayed on the website of the company.
    2. For a book built issue, you can enter upto maximum of 3 (three) bids. The first bid is mandatory. The second and third bids are optional.
    3. Checking the 'Cut Off' option (book built issues only) implies that you are applying for this issue at its cut-off price. After selecting it, the price displayed is the ceiling price. However, once the cut-off price is decided by the Book Running Lead Manager and the Issuer Company, and it happens to be less than the ceiling price, the balance amount is refunded to your bank account.
  6. Select the demat account where you want the shares credited to.
  7. Click Proceed. (Please see the note below for quantity restrictions and details of book building issue)
  8. Preview your order and click Confirm.
  9. Read the issue's Disclaimer and click I Agree. In case you do not wish to agree then you may collect and submit the IPO application in physical form at any of the Lead Managers / Sub â€“ syndicate members office.
  10. In case you do not have sufficient limits, the system will display a pop-up for online hold for the necessary amount to increase your limits. Select a Bank account from where the online hold should be placed. Click the Submit button when done.
  11. Click Place Order.
  12. Preview your order and click on Confirm.
    Once your order has been successfully placed, a confirmation screen displaying a Broker Reference and IPO Application number is displayed. You can place an order upto the specified cut off time (as decided by Angel Broking Ltd). The cut-off time for an issue can be found out from the 'Issue Details' section in the Place Order screen. Angel Broking Ltd, may at its sole discretion withdraw the Product facility either permanently or temporarily for all or a specific Investor.
Note:
  1. The maximum retail and minimum non-institutional investment values can be found out from the 'Issue Details' section in the Place Order screen.
  2. Only retail investors will be allowed to apply at "Cut Off" price (ceiling price).
  3. In case multiple bids are entered, the bid with the highest application value is selected and a funds hold for this amount is required to complete the order.
  4. In case your bid is made at the issue's cut-off price, the funds hold required to complete the order is calculated as the bid quantity multiplied by the issue's ceiling price. Your final allotment may or may not happen at the ceiling price. The actual cut-off price is decided by the Lead manager/s of the issuing company as per the bidding process of SEBI.
  5. In Non ASBA, an order cannot be modified or cancelled. You can modify/cancel only ASBA (Applications Supported by blocked Amount) applications for Book Building and French Auction IPO's in Ordered and Executed status.
  6. In case of any cancellation of application you may contact the respective Registrar to the issue as mentioned in the RHP of the issue.

After your order has been placed

  1. All applications received by us will be assigned an application number. You can lookup the application number for an IPO order from the Order Book. You are requested to quote this number in all your future correspondence with us/ the Registrar to the Issue/the Issuer Company.
  2. In case of Non ASBA applications, the amount placed on hold towards an application will be debited from your account at the end of the day. Under ASBA, the amount will be debited from the bank account on allotment only to the extent of successful allotment of the securities.
  3. The information about bids/applications where holds have been successfully placed in your bank account will be sent to NSE/BSE/ the registrar to the issue/ Issuer company/ Lead manager. However your application/bid will be considered valid only once the hold in your bank account have been successfully debited.
  4. Select a category (NRE Retail/NRO Retail/ASBA NRO Retail/ASBA NRE Retail/Non Institution NRE/Non Institution NRO/ASBA NRO Non Institution/ASBA NRE Non Institution) for NRI category. The issue's details are displayed.

After your order has been placed

  1. On allotment of shares, you will receive "Allotment advice" from the registrar to the issue/ Issuer Company.
  2. The registrar to the Issue will credit the shares in your stipulated demat a/c. on the date of allotment.
  3. The partial / full application amount will be refunded directly to you by the registrar in case of Non ASBA. The cheque will be mailed to your address as per the DP towards full / partial refund. Under the ASBA process, the amount will be debited from bank account only to the extent of successful allotment and balance amount on Hold can be released through the Fund/Release option available else Angel Broking will release the Funds at EOD. Until such allotment, the amount will remain blocked in investors bank account.
  4. The investor/s has to directly liase with the registrar/ Issuer quoting the application number assigned at the time of "Place order". (this is also available in order Book history) Though a complaint w.r.t. to the allotment/ refund may be routed through Angel Broking Ltd. for assistance.
