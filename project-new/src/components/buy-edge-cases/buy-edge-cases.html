Cover Order

What is a Cover Order?

1/4

A Cover order is a 2-in-1 Intraday order consisting of a 'Buy/Sell' order along with a compulsory 'Stop loss' order.

close next

How to use a Stop Loss?

2/4

A 'Stop loss' order helps you limit the losses in case the prices move against your trade. If you are buying a stock, then you will have to add a ‘Sell’ stop-loss. If you have to sell a stock, then you will have to add a ‘Buy’ stop-loss.

close next

What is a Trigger Price?

3/4

‘Trigger price’ indicates the price at which the Stop loss order will get activated/triggered. As soon as the 'Trigger price' is reached, the Stop loss order will get executed at the next Market price.

close next

How Cover Order helps?

4/4
  • Minimize risks
  • Maximize profit
  • Use higher leverage
  • Trade without emotion

Note: Once the buy/sell order is executed, you cannot cancel the Stop loss order. However, you can square off the Cover order at Market price.

What is a Bracket Order?

1/5

A Bracket order is a 3-in-1 Intraday order consisting of a 'Buy/Sell' order along with a compulsory 'Stop loss' order and 'Target' order.

close next

How to use a Stop Loss?

2/5

A 'Stop loss' order helps you limit the losses in case the prices move against your trade. If you are buying a stock, then you will have to add a ‘Sell’ stop-loss. If you have to sell a stock, then you will have to add a ‘Buy’ stop-loss.

close next

What is SL price?

3/5

'SL price' is the price at which your Stop loss order will get activated/triggered. Once the 'SL price' is reached, the Stop loss order will get executed at the next Market price.

close next

What is Target price?

4/5

'Target price' indicates the price at which you want to book your profit. If the profit is booked, then the corresponding Stop loss order is cancelled and if the Stop loss is triggered then the respective Target order is cancelled.

close next

What is Trailing SL Jump Price?

5/5

'Trailing SL Jump Price' indicates the price at which you want your Stop loss price to increase based on the increase in LTP

Note: Once the buy/sell order is executed you cannot cancel the corresponding Stop loss or Target order. However, you can square off the Bracket order at Market price
